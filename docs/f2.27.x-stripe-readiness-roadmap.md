# F2.27.X — Stripe Readiness Roadmap

## 1. Purpose

This document prepares a future Stripe integration for NubeRush. It does not
activate payments and does not introduce any payment code. F2.27.X is
planning and readiness only.

The goal is to capture, in one authoritative place, the current verified
payment state, the boundaries that must hold, the reconciliation work that
must precede any real money movement, and the phased plan by which Stripe
could later be activated. Nothing in this document changes runtime behavior.

This roadmap is subordinate to `docs/f2.27-contract-lock.md`, which already
names this phase ("F2.27.X — Stripe Readiness Roadmap", planning doc only)
and defines the Stripe Readiness Boundary and Compliance Boundary. Where the
contract lock and this document overlap, the contract lock is authoritative
and this document cross-references it rather than restating it.

Live payment processing remains gated on Stripe account approval, the future
Customer App, and explicit legal and compliance review. None of those are in
scope here.

## 2. Current Payment State

The current state was verified by repository diagnosis. As of F2.27.X:

- No Stripe SDK is present in any dependency manifest.
- No payment processor integration of any kind exists.
- No PaymentIntent is created anywhere.
- No payment webhook is implemented or registered.
- No checkout flow exists in any surface.
- No payment capture occurs.
- No refund implementation exists.
- No payout implementation exists.
- No Customer App checkout exists.
- No Driver App delivery or age-verification flow exists.
- No real customer payment flow exists in any part of the Web App.

Existing order totals are operational amounts only. `Order.subtotal_amount`,
`Order.tax_amount`, and `Order.total_amount` are `Numeric(10, 2)` values
recorded for internal operational accounting. They are captured when an order
is created and never represent a processed charge. No external charge is made
against them; `backend/app/domain/orders_rules.py` records real payment
integration as explicitly out of scope and notes that `total_amount` is
captured but no charge is made.

## 4. Current Financial Surfaces

The current earnings and admin finance surfaces are read-only and projected.
Specifically, they are:

- read-only — computed on request from `orders` and `order_items`, with no
  persistence, no `db.add`, and no `commit` in the earnings service;
- projected — derived from internal business rules, not processed payment
  activity;
- internal-accounting only — a base for future billing, not a live ledger;
- not payment-processor-backed — no figure originates from Stripe or any
  processor;
- not payout-backed — no figure represents money paid out to a store;
- not invoice-backed — no figure represents an issued or settled invoice.

The admin and store earnings surfaces already carry a shared disclaimer that
states all three facts: the figures are projected internal accounting, Stripe
payment processing is not yet enabled, and no funds have been charged or paid
out through NubeRush. These disclaimers, and the tests that assert their
presence, must remain in place until a future payments activation phase
explicitly replaces them.

This document does not edit those disclaimers and does not change any earnings
surface.

## 5. Pre-Payment Reconciliation Items

This is the most important section. The following items must be resolved
before any real money moves. They are recorded here as future blocking work;
none of them is implemented in F2.27.X.

1. Commission source of truth (blocking item #1).
   - Diagnosis found a divergence between the commission rate used by the
     earnings surface and the commission rate used by admin settings.
   - The earnings service uses a 20% rate; the admin settings billing section
     uses 5% (500 basis points).
   - This is the single highest-priority reconciliation item and is blocking
     item #1 for any future payment activation.
   - A future phase must decide one authoritative commission source and its
     storage location, then make every surface read from it. No money may be
     captured or settled while two divergent rates coexist.

2. Placeholder delivery fee.
   - The delivery fee is currently a hardcoded constant in the earnings
     service, not modeled order data.
   - Any delivery fee that will ever be charged must become modeled order data
     (a real column with a real value per order) before payment capture.

3. Placeholder tip amount.
   - The tip amount is currently a hardcoded zero constant.
   - Tips cannot remain placeholder values if they are ever charged; they must
     be modeled, validated, and captured per order before payment activation.

4. Missing payment lifecycle.
   - There is no payment status or payment state machine today.
   - Future work must define `payment_status` and the related states, and the
     transitions between them, before Stripe activation.

5. Missing refund, payout, settlement, and reconciliation models.
   - None of these concepts exist in the data model today.
   - They belong to future phases only and must not be introduced here.

## 6. Future Stripe Architecture

The future architecture is described by surface. The current Web App must not
become the checkout surface.

Customer App (future):

- Future owner of customer-facing checkout.
- Future owner of the customer payment flow.

Backend (future):

- Future owner of server-side Stripe API calls.
- Future owner of webhook verification.
- Future owner of payment state transitions.
- Future owner of payment audit and reconciliation.

Admin Web App (current and future):

- Operational and admin visibility only.
- Not the owner of customer checkout.

Store Web App (current and future):

- Store operational visibility only.
- May display future payment and order state.
- Must not capture payments.

Driver App (future):

- Future owner of delivery proof and age-verification workflows.
- Not part of F2.27.X.

The Web App is the admin and store operations surface. It is not, and must not
become, the customer checkout surface. Checkout and customer payment belong to
the future Customer App.

## 8. Future Configuration Requirements

This section documents where future Stripe configuration may eventually live.
No configuration is added now.

Future possible locations:

- `backend/.env.example` — for server-side Stripe placeholders only, and only
  after a future config phase is explicitly approved. Server-side Stripe
  secrets follow the existing server-only-secret pattern already used for the
  QuickBooks and FDA blocks: blank default, never exposed to the frontend,
  never logged, real values only in the deployment secret manager.
- `render.yaml` — for Stripe secrets declared with `sync: false`, only after a
  future config phase is explicitly approved, mirroring the existing
  secret-handling convention where no real secret lives in the repository.
- `web/.env.example` — only for future publishable or browser-safe Stripe
  configuration, and only if a future Customer App architecture explicitly
  requires it. No server-side Stripe secret may ever appear here, because Vite
  inlines `VITE_*` values into the public bundle.

For F2.27.X specifically:

- No STRIPE_* placeholders are added.
- No secrets are added.
- No render configuration is changed.
- No Vite payment configuration is added.

## 9. Future Webhook Requirements

When a webhook is eventually implemented in a future phase, it must satisfy:

- signature verification of every inbound event;
- raw request body preservation for signature verification;
- idempotency, so duplicate deliveries are processed once;
- an event ledger recording received events;
- replay safety against out-of-order and repeated deliveries;
- well-defined state transitions driven by verified events;
- explicit failure handling for unverifiable or unexpected events;
- an audit trail of webhook-driven changes;
- no secret leakage in logs, errors, or stored data;
- tests built on mocked events rather than live calls.

No webhook is implemented in this phase.

## 10. Future Checkout / Customer App Boundary

The product boundary is:

- Web App = admin and store operations.
- Customer App = future customer ordering and checkout.
- Driver App = future delivery execution, proof, and age-verification
  workflows.

Checkout belongs to a future Customer App phase, not to F2.27.X. The Web App
does not gain a checkout surface as part of this roadmap.

## 11. Future Refund, Payout, and Reconciliation Requirements

When payments are activated, future work must define:

- the refund lifecycle;
- the cancellation and refund policy;
- payout reporting;
- settlement reconciliation;
- the transition of earnings figures into a real earnings ledger;
- admin and store visibility into refunds, payouts, and settlement;
- audit logs for all money-affecting events;
- support workflows for disputes and corrections.

None of this is implemented now.

## 12. Compliance and Legal Gates

Stripe activation cannot be a purely technical decision. Before any payment
activation, the following gates must be satisfied:

- legal review;
- compliance review;
- Stripe account approval;
- product and category eligibility review;
- smoke-shop policy review;
- an age-verification plan;
- a refund and cancellation policy;
- a delivery legality review if delivery is later activated;
- preservation of human-gated regulatory enforcement.

The existing regulatory model is human-gated: automated ingestion may create
notices, signals, and alerts only, and every lifecycle action requires a human
admin decision. That posture must be preserved. No payment or delivery
activation may introduce automatic compliance enforcement.

## 13. Public Copy and Brand Boundary

The brand and copy boundary for this roadmap is:

- This roadmap may mention Stripe because it lives under `docs/`.
- Public and legal copy must not mention Stripe yet.
- Marketing copy must not change.
- Existing payment disclaimers must not be removed.
- The product must not imply payments are live before activation.

The public legal pages are test-guarded against rendering third-party brand
names, including Stripe. This document stays in `docs/` and does not introduce
Stripe naming into any public, legal, or marketing surface.

## 14. Phased Future Activation Plan

The following phased plan describes how Stripe could later be activated. Every
phase is future work and outside F2.27.X.

- Stripe Phase 0 — Legal, compliance, and account approval.
- Stripe Phase 1 — Config and secrets readiness.
- Stripe Phase 2 — Payment data model.
- Stripe Phase 3 — Checkout contract.
- Stripe Phase 4 — Stripe backend integration.
- Stripe Phase 5 — Webhook ledger and reconciliation.
- Stripe Phase 6 — Customer App checkout UI.
- Stripe Phase 7 — Refunds, payouts, and earnings ledger.
- Stripe Phase 8 — Production go-live checklist.

Every future phase requires its own diagnosis, architecture, gameplan,
implementation prompt, validation prompt, and pass/fail report. No phase may
begin before the reconciliation items in Section 5 and the gates in Section 12
are satisfied for the work it touches.
